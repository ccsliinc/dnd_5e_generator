Character Appearance

A sturdy hill dwarf with weathered tan skin and keen amber eyes that constantly scan the treeline. His brown beard is thick and well-kept, often with bits of forest debris caught in it. He moves with the quiet confidence of someone who has spent years navigating wild places, and the faint smell of pine and bear musk follows him.

Character Backstory

Thorek was a simple woodsman living on the edge of the great forest, making his living as a carpenter and guide. One fateful autumn, an orc warband descended upon his village. During the attack, he discovered a wounded bear cub whose mother had been slain by the orcs. Together, they fought off the raiders. Thorek wielded his grandfather's warhammer while the young bear battled with surprising ferocity.

The village elder proclaimed them both heroes, and from that day forward, Thorek and the bear became inseparable companions. He named the bear Ironjaw for his powerful bite, and now they travel the land together as defenders of the common folk.

Allies & Organizations
The Village of Millhaven

The village Thorek and Ironjaw saved from the orc warband. The villagers consider him a hero and will always offer shelter and aid. He also has connections with local woodcutters and the trappers guild.

Description
This ancient iron ring bears intricate carvings of intertwining vines punctuated by tiny animal tracks — wolf paws, bear claws, eagle talons, and deer hooves that seem to circle endlessly around the band. When worn by a Beast Master, the etchings emit a faint green luminescence, and the ranger's animal companion's eyes occasionally gleam with the same emerald light. The ring feels warm to the touch when beasts are near, and observers swear the carved vines slowly shift when viewed from the corner of one's eye.

Lore & History
Forged in the First Age by the Archdruid Kelthanas Ironbark, these rings were created to honor the rangers who stood beside their animal companions during the War of Thorns. Only seven were made, each quenched in the Moonwell of Eternal Spring and blessed by the Great Bear Spirit. This ring was thought lost when Ranger-Captain Thorne Grimbear fell defending Silverwood Pass — until you recovered it.
— From the Archives of the Emerald Enclave
